Get Started

Note for Hawaiʻi visitors

  • Laynets are a type of gill net used from shore or a boat and they hold strong cultural and traditional significance in Hawaiʻi.
  • You do not need a lay net permit for polefishing or diving.
  • Their use comes with additional regulations and is not recommended for beginners or casual fishing.

The Updated Permit

  • Amendments to the lay net rules (HAR 13-75-12.4) took effect on March 11, 2023, replacing the previous lay net registration process.
  • One permit number is assigned to each permit holder and can be used to tag every lay net they own, with no need for different numbers for different nets.
  • Allows permit holders to obtain additional nets at any time without registering them individually.

Permit Validity

Each permit is valid for one year, from July 1 to June 30, and can be renewed annually

Cost

  • HI Residents - Free
  • Non Residents - $25
  • Additional processing fee at checkout
